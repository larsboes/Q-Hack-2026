# (Build towards) Digital Independence & Digital Sovereignty

## Overview

At Exxeta AG, we believe that true digital transformation can only be sustainable when it is
built on a foundation of independence, transparency, and sovereignty. In an era where critical
digital infrastructure, cloud services, and AI platforms are increasingly dominated by a handful
of global tech giants — many of which are bound by foreign legislation and geopolitical
interests — the question of who controls your data, your services, and your digital future has
never been more urgent.

Recent geopolitical developments have made painfully clear how vulnerable businesses,
governments, and individuals become when their digital operations depend on services and
platforms beyond their own jurisdiction. For the European Union in particular, digital
sovereignty has risen to the top of the agenda — across government institutions, critical
industries, and the private sector alike. Exxeta AG, as a European technology and consulting
company, is deeply committed to helping organizations navigate this challenge and reclaim
control over their digital ecosystems. (https://exxeta.com/blog/interview-digitale-souveraenitaet-steuerbarer-reifegrad)

That is why this year's Q-Hack challenge invites you to be part of the solution.

We challenge you to build towards Digital Independence and Digital Sovereignty — developing
innovative tools, platforms, and systems that reduce reliance on foreign-controlled
technologies and empower users, organizations, and institutions to understand, manage, and
protect their digital assets. Solutions should prioritize locally run services, open-source
technologies, and sovereign infrastructure wherever possible.

The challenge is intentionally open to diverse approaches:

• AI-focused teams might build a fully local Agentic AI Assistant, leveraging local Large
Language Models (LLMs), local speech recognition, and on-premises infrastructure —
demonstrating that powerful AI experiences no longer require dependency on foreign
cloud providers.

• Business- and data-oriented teams might develop a Data Exploitation Awareness
Platform — an application that analyses Terms & Conditions and Privacy Policies to
expose how user data is collected, retained, and monetized, while calculating the
hidden financial cost of "free" digital services: how you truly pay with your data.

Regardless of the direction your team chooses, the guiding principle remains the same: use as
many independent, local, and sovereign technologies as possible, and build something that
demonstrates a credible path away from Big-Tech dependency.

At Exxeta, we are not just posing this challenge theoretically — it reflects the real work we do
every day alongside our clients. We see firsthand how organizations are waking up to the risks
of digital dependency, and we are actively helping them chart a course toward resilience and
self-determination. Q-Hack 2026 is your opportunity to contribute to this mission.

We are excited to support each team throughout their creative and technical journey, and we
can't wait to see the solutions you build. Let's shape a more sovereign digital future —
together.


## Target Group

• Students interested in artificial intelligence, data privacy, and digital rights

• Students with skills in web and mobile development, backend infrastructure, and AI
service integration

• Aspiring entrepreneurs and developers looking to create impactful solutions for digital
sovereignty and data protection


## An Application Idea – Data Exploitation Awareness Platform:

An application that analyses Terms & Conditions and Privacy Policies to extract data
collection, retention, and monetization practices using local AI models

• A personalized data valuation system that calculates the financial worth of user data
based on screen time, engagement, and industry benchmarks

• A user-friendly interface displaying data collection scope, financial value assessment,
privacy risk scores, and recommendations for privacy-friendly alternatives


## Technology Stack Suggestions

We encourage teams to freely choose their tech stack up to their likings or past experiences.
Below participants will find a list of recommended technologies:

• Local AI Model: LLaMA 3, Mistral, or Phi-3-mini via Ollama or LM Studio to process
documents locally without cloud dependencies

• Web Development: React or Svelte combined with Python FastAPI or Node.js for a
responsive frontend and backend

• Data Visualization: D3.js or Chart.js to display data footprint scores and valuation
insights


## Deliverables

- Next.js or other JavaScript frameworks in combination with Spring Boot and MongoDB for developing a responsive and robust frontend and backend in a classical service-oriented architecture.

### 🧪 Evaluation Criteria

A total of **20 points** can be awarded across three categories:

#### 💼 Business Criteria (0–8 Points)

##### Degree of Innovation (0–5 Points)
- How **novel** and **creative** is the solution?  
- Is there a clear **differentiation factor** compared to existing solutions?

##### Business Impact & Scalability (0–3 Points)
- How **realistic** is its implementation in practice?  
- How **scalable** is the solution?  
- Does the solution have **potential to create a business impact**?

#### 🛠️ Technical Criteria (0–8 Points)

##### Technical Implementation (0–5 Points)
- How well is the technical implementation done? → **Code quality, architecture**  
- Are relevant/required **technologies used effectively**?  
- Is there a **functioning MVP**?

##### UX & Design (0–3 Points)
- Is the user interface **(UI) intuitive and visually appealing**?  
- Was user experience **(UX) taken into account** during development?

#### 🎤 Pitch & Presentation (0–4 Points)
- Is the **idea clearly and structured explained** so that the value of the solution is effectively communicated?  
- Is the **presentation visually appealing** and professional?  
- Are potential **questions or challenges well addressed**?
