The Q-Hack is an innovation competition that brings together students, developers, and creative minds to develop innovative solutions for real-world problems in a short time. Our partner companies play a key role by providing exciting challenges that serve as the foundation for participants' projects.

This guideline is designed to help partner companies formulate a clear and effective challenge. It serves as a reference to ensure that the task is relevant, engaging, and feasible.

#### Core Principles:

Open, creative problem: No predetermined solution—room for innovative approaches

Practical output: A presentable prototype should be developed within 24 hours

Potential for further development: The results should have the potential for future implementation

Optional company involvement: Companies can introduce their own challenges as long as they remain open to different solution approaches

#### Q-Hack Key Facts

There are a total of 5 challenges

Each challenge is tackled by 6 teams, with 4-5 hackers per team

The hackers have a total of 24 hours to both develop/build their solution and prepare their pitch

The winners of each challenge are selected by the respective companies and then compete against each other on the main stage

The entire hackathon and Q-Hack Pitch Battle are conducted in English

#### Transparent Criteria - same for all 5 challenges

Divided into 3 evaluation categories: Business Criteria, Technical Criteria, Pitch & Presentation

#### Business Criteria (0-8 Points)

#### Degree of innovation (0-5 Points)

#### Business Impact & Scalability (0-3 Points)

#### Technical Criteria (0-8 Points)

#### Technical Implementation (0-5 Points)

#### UX & Design (0-3 Points)

#### Pitch & Presentation (0-4 Points)

Is the idea clearly and structured explained so that the value of the solution effectively communicated?

Is the presentation visually appealing and professional?

Are potential questions or challenges well addressed?

#### Structure Guidelines

Task for partner companies: Create a suitable document based on this structural guideline

Evaluation criteria is defined by Q-Hack team - see above

#### Problem Statement:

What problem should be solved? (Formulate briefly and concisely – but leave enough room for creativity)

Why is this problem relevant? (Industry relevance, current context)

What challenges currently exist?

#### Data & Resources:

Will datasets or APIs be provided?

Is there any specific tech stack or tools that should/can be used?

Are there any constraints or special conditions to consider?

#### Expected Outcomes:

A functional prototype/MVP should be developed.

A presentation should be prepared

Should additional deliverables be created?

Are there requirements for technical feasibility?

#### Target Audience of the Challenge:

Which type of participants should feel particularly addressed?

Are specific prior knowledge or fields of study relevant? (e.g., Data Science, AI)
